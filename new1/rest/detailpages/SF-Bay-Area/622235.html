 "Amazing!!!!" by Jane Doe Eats - Seattle (120 reviews)
December 12, 2008 - Really likes it - Mingalaba was immediately added to a tiny number of my favorite restaurants last night. The small, friendly place is nestled among a lovely promenade of shops on Burlingame Avenue and evokes immediate peace and tranquility just two steps into the front door. The smiles on the staff were warm and genuine. Couples were swooning, tables full of g-friends were exchanging gifts over some mystery celebration, parents with adorable kids in tow were picking up take-out. I'm really big on experiencing a place with all my senses and Mingalaba delighted them all effortlessly. We ordered items as unique as possible from the menu, including the Lap Pat Dok (tea leaf salad), Palatha (Burmese crispy pancake) Burmese style curry lamb, House Special Noodle, Naked Spicy Eggplant, Mango Lassi's and some crazy wild dessert dish with fresh coconut, tapioca, jello, ice cream, etc. . The meal was amazing. The Tea Leaf salad started out as a painter's palate of items, crispy toasted garlic, peanuts, cabbage, sesame seeds, ground tea leaves and other items. The server described all the ingredients and lightly tossed them together table-side. The same service was applied to the noodle dish which dazzled us with a complex variety of flavors including a bright zing imparted by delicate threads of fresh lime leaves. The curry lamb arrived spicy and tender in large chunks bubbling in a nice piece of pottery. I swooned over the eggplant, flash crisped on the edges and lightly dressed in a sweet and sour glaze. I was even impressed with the rice, which in most places is a boring and unhealthy ball of nutrient-stripped white fluff. There are several types to choose from here and our pick, the Saffron rice, was generously seasoned and dotted with dried fruit, peas and carrots. The portions were extremely generous and I regretted that I didn't have a frig in my hotel room so my dinner companion took home all the goodiesÔÅå. The mango lassi was superior to those I've had in other places, light in consistency but intense with mango goodness. The desert was a 4th of July celebration of colors, textures and flavors. By that time I was way beyond full so I watched the large glass of sweets disappear at the spoon of my dining companion and admired the creative tableware, serving utensils, unique chandeliers and decorum. Everything about Mingalaba was just wonderful. I felt so healthy and happy in there with the fresh ingredients and intricate dishes. When I get home to Seattle I'm going to ferret out our local Burmese restaurants (if we have any) and hope they are as fabulous as Mingalaba. Put this place on your list if you want to dazzle your palate with no regrets and make some fond memories.
