 "For tourists AND locals" by Lisa Guinn (72 reviews)
February 17, 2009 - Likes it - I take my out-of-town friends here when they want to go to Fisherman's Wharf.  I like the sand dabs and the clam chowder.  Friends have raved about the cioppino.    The food here is good -- unlike some of the tourist traps.  They don't take reservations, so put your name on the list BEFORE you get hungry!  Expect to wait, but maybe you can wander around the wharf for a bit in the meantime.

 "Bloody Mary's & Halibut Chops" by Jane Doe Eats - Seattle (121 reviews)
December 11, 2008 - Likes it - Scoma's is cool. The old carpet near the bar smells like death but the views, staff and cuisine are very enjoyable.  The Bloody Mary's in particular are so tasty I could drink them to unconsciousness.  Just as yummy as those at Elliott’s Oyster Bar in Seattle.  Scoma’s staff know show to shuck oysters without popping them, gently broils the fish to perfection without overcooking and has superb staff that exude genuine friendliness and keep track of courses.  Last time I was there I relished raw oysters and clams, perfectly poached shrimp, a big thick halibut chop and swilled 3-4 B.Mary's.  Good Times!
