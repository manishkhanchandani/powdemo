Message from Rudy's Can'T Fail Cafe

Rudy's is now open from 7AM to 1AM EVERY DAY!

  • Open until 1AM EVERY NIGHT!!!
  • Breakfast served all day
  • Great weekend Brunch menu

Rudy's Can't Fail Cafe is the hot spot in Emeryville for breakfast, lunch, brunch and dinner. Open until 1AM every night! This colorful cafe serves great omelets, pancakes, burgers and traditional diner specialties. Rudy's features Peet's Coffee and Tea. Try the meatloaf, fish and chips, or the classic Reuben sandwich. Beware of the highly addictive hollandaise sauce. Handcrafted beers on tap include New Belgium's Fat Tire Amber Ale and Blue Paddle Pilsner. The cafe is frequented by the East Bay's alternative music crowd and local business people. Families also enjoy the kid-friendly atmosphere. Reservations are recommended for parties of 8 or more. Come enjoy our classic diner with a bit of an edge.

June 02, 2009 - Likes it - Don't let the small building fool you as you walk in and get welcomed by loud music and tons of customers. I visited twice, both times very busy. Even at 1030 at night on a Monday night it was hopping. I received poor service however it seemed as if they were severely understaffed. The food was great however, how hard is it to make a good turkey reuban? They did comp my beer because of the service however. Good place if you're looking for a sandwich or tofu dishes. They also have a wide range of specials everyday and they serve breakfast all day long. If your looking for a good late night dinner go to rudy's, they're open until 1 am every night.

 "Wow, amazing fries and ambiance" by Amber Gregory (15 reviews)
March 08, 2009 - Likes it - I went to Rudy's in 2007 with two friends from college, and we had a great time. It was not crowded, our waiter was attentive and had awesome tattooed sleeves, and the food was great. I had a veggie taco salad, sans sour cream, and it came in one of those lovely fried tortilla bowls that I absolutely cannot resist. The Fat Tire on tap was a bonus, and my friend insisted I try the french fries--which were AMAZING! We were talking about them long after we'd left the restaurant.
I live in the city, so the location is a bit inconvenient for me, but I really wish I'd known about this place when I lived in the east bay!
