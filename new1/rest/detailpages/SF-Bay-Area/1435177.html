 "I love this place!" by Prideaux (1 review)
June 07, 2009 - Likes it - I love this place. The dining room is warm and inviting. The owner is always there at dinner and provides wonderful customer service every single time my girlfriends and I have eaten there. Most importantly, the food is exceptionally delicious. I highly recommend the Samosa, Chicken Tandoore, Daal, Chicken Tikka Masala, Sindhi Biryani, and my personal favorite, Saag Paneer. The food always arrives promptly and perfectly prepared. I have never eaten a bad dish here yet, and to date I've dined here more than a dozen times. I've eaten at quite a few restaurants serving Indian/Pakistani cuisine in the city, but none so far comes close to the quality and low prices of Tikka Masala. If you enjoy Indian/Pakistani cuisine or want to try it out for the first time, this is the perfect place to go.
Pros: Prices, service, food.
Cons: The neighborhood needs to be "cleaned up." And what I mean by that is the city management along with the police needs to get the drug dealers out.

 "Excellent!" by Susan (2 reviews)
May 08, 2009 - Really likes it - Amongst the many Indian/Pakistani restaurants I have eaten at, this restaurant is certainly at the top of the list in both the quality of food and price. I've been to about a dozen Indian/Pakistani restaurants and this restaurant is outstanding. I chose this restaurant based on Yelp's positive reviews since I was visiting from out of town and was unfamiliar with local restaurants. Choosing Tikka Masala turned out to be a great move, surpassing my high expectations. The Samosas were inexpensive and freshly made. The naan was fluffy and perfectly cooked. Usually naan is burned in many areas, yet this naan was burn-free. My boyfriend ordered Chicken Tikka Masala and Biryani, which tasted much better as at the restaurants that charge almost double for the same thing. The owner was concerned about the comfort of his guests, and seemed eager to please. We ordered extra naan, and it was out almost immediately, despite the packed house he was simultaneously attending to. At the end I was offered complimentary tea, which was an extra perk for such an already great meal.
