 "WORST MEAL I NEVER GOT TO EAT!!" by wongwahching (4 reviews)
May 09, 2009 - Doesn't like it - We had heard many a good thing about the Slanted Door. My wife being a chef and I being and architect attending the National AIA convention, decided we wanted to give one of San Francisco's supposedly "trendiest" spots a try.
We booked our reservations a week in advance, and were looking forward to a nice night on the town.
We arrived at 7:30pm, a full hour ahead of schedule. We announced ourselves to the hostess that we were present and reminded her of our 8:30pm reservation. She told us to return at our scheduled time.
We returned after an hour at exactly 8:30 and were told that are seats were about ready and that it would be about a 10 minute wait. As 8:45 rolled passed, I saw couple after couple being seated. At 8:50pm I asked how are seats were coming along which she replied that they were on their way and jokingly replied that she "knew it was fustrating". At 9:20 my wife, who is pregnant, and now standing for 50 minutes decided she had finally had enough and walked over to the hostess and bluntly said..."do you have our seats ready"... to which the Twit responds...."No."... We left.
Don't ever eat here. This level of service is tantamount to criminal. Our reservation was not honoured and our patronage was not required. They had essentially wasted our patience and more importantly, our time. Having lived in New York City, Hong Kong, and Toronto, and being a patron of fine restaurants in all three great cities and many restaurants in San Francisco, I can tell you without pause, that the single customer service requirement of any restaurant (high end or not) is the honouring of reservation. It is the inability to do so, that is the most telling of this restaurants lack of professionalism and service, and it will without doubt be this restaurant downfall.

3 people like this review - Recommend

 "Slanted Door, mainstream with little to brag about" by Handsome Bob's caretaker (3 reviews)
March 23, 2009 - Doesn't like it - We were traveling and read all about the Slanted Door on Urban Spoon, how it was in the top 10 places in SFO to go. Being from Seattle, we are very lucky to have such great choices at home as well. We arrived at our appointed time having made reservations via the web, (Very good idea!) only to be treated as if we were scum off the street by the hostess... We were no different then the urban gen x well dressed people that seemed to fill the place up wall to wall... After being somewhat annoyed by her, we sat down to a decent table and great waiter. All was good and lots of perceived deliciousness on the menu, food arrived and was mediocre at best... The only item I could even recommend was off the starter menu, Dikon Rice Cakes. The rest was bland, ugly and without any zest one would hope for in this day of amazing chefs and restaurateurs... I would give this place a 6 out of 10 and wouldn't recommend it to anyone except those who either love the Cheesecake Factory or a clone of Wild Ginger in Seattle, food for the masses without any flair. Too bad since it looks like it lost its roots from what I have read...
