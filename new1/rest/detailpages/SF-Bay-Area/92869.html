 "Friendly, grounded and authentic" by Robert (2 reviews)
January 25, 2009 - Likes it - We greatly enjoyed Utopia Cafe. Distinctively, from the outside, it was full of chinese families enjoying themselves eating in an informal environment. We were quickly seated by friendly staff at one of the close-packed, formica tables and tea and menus came quickly.
The food was delicious, much better than comparable SF Chinatown meals and also chinese meals in London (that we know well). Orange peel chicken stands out in our memories as having been particularly good: hot, sticky, battered, well but not over-flavoured. Delicious.
This was a really friendly, down-to-earth place where we would eat all the time if its was a few thousand miles closer to home.

 "Out of the Way, Neighborhood Feel" by teebodo (1 review)
October 26, 2007 - Likes it - In an attempt to avoid the touristy spots on Grant (the Four Seas? Completely awful food) my wife and I decided to venture a block or two away from the glare. We were drawn toward what sounded like traditional operatic singing coming from the top floor of a community center.
Across the street, behind a steamy plate glass window, sat the unassuming Utopia Cafe, almost full or patrons. There were a few "family-style" large round tables (occupied) and the remaining standard four-seaters. Looked like it was a popular place with locals and families (a broad range of ages). All good signs to us, so we went in.
The decor will not win any awards, and if florescent lights give you a headache, stay away. However, we were seated quickly, without fuss, and a pot of tea and the menus appeared right away.
The Utopia Cafe is supposed to be well known for their clay pots, so I ordered the spare ribs (the method involves cooking rice with a topping, the rice gets crispy along the sides of the pot and lends a nice crunchy texture to the meal). my wife was in the mood for shrimp.
Her meal came with sauteed onions and green peppers, but we wanted another vegetable. We had spotted a dish of greens (broccoli rabe?) that another table had ordered, so we asked for that. We don't know what it is called, but it was fresh and cooked very well, with a slight crispness. The shrimp was not overcooked (my pet peeve) with a good sauce which could have had a bit more heat (the menu said it was spicy, but we think it got toned down due to non-native factors). My clay pot spareribs were tasty and the rice and ribs were good, although it could have used a sauce or something (an assumption, I thought the ribs might be saucy).
A family was sitting behind us with a little boy, parents and grandparents that kept us entertained. The grandmother managed to showcase her grandchild through the whole restaurant stopping to say hi to other patrons whom she knew. A few customers came in to collect their pick-up order and managed to recognize seated patrons and stopped to chat. Overall, it was a very nice place and we would go again or recommend.
