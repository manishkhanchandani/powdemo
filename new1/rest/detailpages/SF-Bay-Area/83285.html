 "Best breakfast I've ever had" by Matthew (32 reviews)
December 22, 2008 - Really likes it - After reading reviews, I arrived at around 11am. The 45-minute wait to get in went by quickly as I talked with other tourists about what they'd done while they were in the city. Had the Raspberry Oat Pancakes from the specials board. It was easily the best breakfast I've ever had. Service was exemplary -- coffee constantly freshened up, large orange juice brought immediately, etc.
As I pigged out, I found myself wondering how the entire staff wasn't obese from employee meals. It's because they are so busy! Constantly turning tables over so they can get people in off the street. You can blame the restaurant for the long waits, but blame me. I wanted to savor every bite before I went back to Seattle. I ordered a Pumpkin Chocolate Chip muffin to go and had it for breakfast the next morning. I know that my next visit to San Francisco won't be complete without going to Dottie's.
Quick hint: show up around 15-20 minutes before they open. I did that the next time I went and got in when they opened at 7:30. Oh, and the neighborhood isn't as bad as I've read in other reviews. I think people that post those reviews are just trying to keep the lines short or something.
Sidenote: The readerboard that was posted under the menu portion of Urbanspoon for Dottie's changes on a daily basis. This is just to give you an idea of what kind of pastries they might have. There is another readerboard (that can be seen on sites like Flickr) that include different kinds of pancakes, french toasts and eggs. That white reader board (again, not seen here) is what you want to order your entree off of -- not the menu that sits at the table. If no one posts a picture of it soon, I'll take a photo of it the next time I am in town.
